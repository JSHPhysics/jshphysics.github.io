How to use

Step 1 of 5
What is a clock cipher?
A clock cipher maps 12 letters onto the positions of a clock face — 12, 1, 2 … 11. To send a secret message, replace each letter with its clock position number. To read one, do the reverse. Rotating the wheel creates a different mapping, acting as the cipher key.
Position  12  → letter  A
Position   1  → letter  B
Position   2  → letter  C
… and so on around the face
Step 2 of 5
Assign letters to the wheel
Use the Cipher Letters panel on the right. Type one letter per row — the cursor advances automatically so you can fill all 12 positions quickly. The number beside each row is its clock position (12, 1, 2 … 11). Drag the ⠿ handle on any row to swap two letters around.
12  [A]  1  [B]  2  [C]  3  [D]  4  [E]  5  [F]  …
Letters appear live on the clock face as you type
Step 3 of 5
Rotate the wheel to set the key
Click and drag the clock face to spin it — it snaps to the nearest hour position when you release. Use the ◀ ▶ buttons to nudge one hour at a time. The fixed numbers in the outer ring never move, so you can always see which letter is currently under each position. Hit ↺ Reset to return to 0°.
Rotate +2 hours (60°) with A–F assigned:
12 → C   1 → D   2 → E   3 → F …
The rotation is the shared secret between sender and receiver
Step 4 of 5
Decode a message
Type (or paste) a sequence of numbers 1–12 into the Message box. Each number is instantly replaced by the letter currently sitting at that clock position. Spaces and punctuation pass through unchanged, so you can include them freely between your numbers.
Input:  3 1 20 5
Output:  D B ? F
(· shown for positions with no letter assigned)
Step 5 of 5
Encode a message
Type plain letters into the Message box instead. Any letter that appears on the wheel is replaced by its current clock-position number. Letters not on the wheel are left as-is. Rotate the wheel to a different key and the whole output updates instantly — making it easy to compare encodings at different offsets.
Input:  CAFE
Output:  3 1 6 5
Rotate the wheel — the numbers change automatically
